🛡️ Pension Deduction Rules

Note: Passive Pension Income is 100% Exempt:

CPP and EI premiums are never deducted from passive retirement income, including CPP benefits, OAS, GIS, or private/corporate pension payouts.

If You Choose to Work While Collecting:

CPP (Age 60–65): Deductions from employment earnings are mandatory and build a Post-Retirement Benefit (PRB).
CPP (Age 65–70): Voluntary. You can opt out of CPP deductions on earnings by filing CRA form CPT30.

Corporate Coordination

Defined Benefit (DB) pension schemes in BC coordinate closely with public programs. The Bridge Benefit and Temporary Annuity programs are designed to automatically end on the first of the month following your 65th birthday.
