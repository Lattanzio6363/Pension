🛡️ Pension Deduction Rules

Are CPP & EI deducted from passive pension income?

No. Passive Pension Income is 100% Exempt:

CPP and EI premiums are never deducted from passive retirement income, including CPP retirement benefits, OAS, GIS, or private/corporate pension payouts.

If You Choose to Work While Collecting a Pension:

CPP (Age 60–65): Deductions from your employment earnings are mandatory and go towards building a Post-Retirement Benefit (PRB).
CPP (Age 65–70): Voluntary. You can opt out of CPP deductions on your earnings by filing CRA form CPT30.
CPP (Age 70+): Contributions stop entirely; no deductions are allowed on any earnings.
EI (No Age Limit): There is no age cut-off for EI. If you earn insurable employment income while retired, EI premiums must be deducted.
